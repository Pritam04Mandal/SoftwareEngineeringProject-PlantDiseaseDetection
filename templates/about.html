Leaf diseases in plants can have a significant impact on agricultural production, resulting in decreased yield and quality of crops. Currently, farmers rely on manual inspection and diagnosis of plant diseases, which can be time-consuming, prone to errors, and may lead to the delay of the appropriate treatment.

To address this issue, a software engineering project is proposed to develop a leaf disease detection system using artificial intelligence (AI). The system will be designed to analyze images of plant leaves and identify the presence of diseases accurately and quickly, providing farmers with early warning of plant health problems. The primary objective of this project is to develop a software application that utilizes AI techniques such as machine learning and computer vision to automate the detection and diagnosis of leaf diseases in plants. The system will be designed to identify common leaf diseases that affect crops, such as powdery mildew, rust, and blight.

The proposed system will provide farmers with an efficient and reliable tool to detect leaf diseases in plants, enabling them to take the necessary actions to prevent the spread of diseases and ensure the health of their crops. The system's success will depend on its ability to accurately and efficiently analyze large volumes of plant images while providing farmers with actionable information in real-time.
